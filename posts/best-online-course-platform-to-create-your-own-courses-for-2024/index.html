Best online course platform to create your own courses for 2024

November 29, 2023 • business

In a world where learning and development are increasingly shifting online, the right platform can be a game-changer for both educators and learners. Whether you’re an aspiring course creator, a professional seeking to upskill, or just a curious mind eager to explore new knowledge, this guide is tailored for you. We’ve meticulously researched and tested various platforms to bring you the top picks that stand out in terms of features, user experience, and overall value. So, dive in with us as we explore the cream of the crop in online education platforms, tailored to meet the diverse needs of the 2024 digital learning landscape!

What Are the Best Online Course Platforms?

Thinkific

Thinkific is a comprehensive online course platform that allows creators to develop, market, and sell their courses with complete control over their branding and content. It’s designed to be user-friendly, catering to both beginners and experienced course creators. With Thinkific, you can build a variety of courses, create custom websites for your content, and engage with your audience through quizzes, surveys, and discussions.

Special Features:

  • Full customization of course sites

  • Drag-and-drop course builder

  • Integrated payment processing

  • Quizzes and surveys for student engagement

  • Membership and subscription options

  • Advanced data tracking and reporting tools

Pros:

  1. Total Control: Offers complete control over branding and course structure.

  2. Ease of Use: User-friendly interface for both instructors and students.

  3. Customization: Highly customizable course pages and content.

  4. Marketing Tools: Includes built-in tools for marketing and selling courses.

  5. Supports Diverse Content: Hosts various types of content including videos, text, PDFs, and quizzes.

Cons:

  1. Limited Marketing Features: While it has marketing tools, they may not be as advanced as some competitors.

  2. Transaction Fees: Depending on the plan, there might be transaction fees.

  3. Limited Course Interaction Options: While there are quizzes and surveys, the interaction options might be limited compared to more specialized platforms.

  4. Email Marketing Integration: Requires third-party tools for more advanced email marketing campaigns.

Overall, Thinkific is an excellent choice for those looking to have a high degree of control over their online courses, with a focus on ease of use and course presentation.

Try Thinkific

Kajabi

Kajabi is a robust platform designed for entrepreneurs and educators to create, market, and sell online courses. It’s more than just a course creation tool; it also offers capabilities for building websites, marketing automation, and a suite of tools for memberships, podcasts, and coaching programs.

Special Features:

  • Podcast Hosting: Unique feature for hosting and integrating podcasts.

  • Advanced Automation: Enables sophisticated marketing and sales automation.

  • Mobile App: Offers a mobile app for both course creators and students for on-the-go access.

  • High-Quality Video Hosting: Provides robust hosting capabilities for high-definition course videos.

Pros:

  1. All-in-One Platform: Integrates course creation, marketing, and sales in one place.

  2. High Customization: Offers extensive customization options for courses and websites.

  3. Marketing Tools: Includes advanced marketing features like email automation and sales funnels.

  4. Membership and Community Building: Supports creating membership sites and communities around your courses.

  5. Analytics and Reporting: Provides detailed analytics for tracking student progress and sales.

Cons:

  1. Pricing: Higher cost compared to some other platforms, making it less accessible for beginners.

  2. Complexity: The range of features can be overwhelming for new users.

  3. Limited Course Interaction: Fewer options for interactive course elements like quizzes and assignments.

Kajabi stands out for those who want a comprehensive solution with strong marketing and sales tools, ideal for professionals looking to scale their online education business.

Try Kajibi

Teachable

Teachable is an intuitive online course platform that empowers experts, educators, and entrepreneurs to create and sell online courses. Its user-friendly interface makes it easy to design courses, upload content, and engage with students. Teachable provides a range of tools to build a comprehensive learning experience, including video lectures, quizzes, and interactive discussions.

Special Features:

  • Customizable course websites

  • Integrated payment processing

  • Advanced analytics for tracking student progress

  • Marketing tools and affiliate programs

  • Flexible course pricing options

  • Mobile-friendly interface

Pros:

  1. User-Friendly Design: Ideal for beginners, with an easy-to-navigate dashboard.

  2. Customization Options: Offers extensive customization for branding and course design.

  3. Integrated Payment Gateways: Simplifies the process of receiving payments.

  4. Comprehensive Analytics: Provides detailed insights into student engagement and course performance.

  5. Strong Community and Support: Access to a large community of creators and responsive customer support.

Cons:

  1. Limited Quiz Features: The quiz functionality is basic compared to other platforms.

  2. Transaction Fees: Depending on the plan, transaction fees can be a bit high.

  3. Limited Marketing Tools: While there are some marketing tools, they might not be sufficient for advanced users.

  4. No Built-in Email Marketing: Relies on integrations for email marketing campaigns.

Teachable stands out for its ease of use and comprehensive features, making it a great choice for those new to online course creation or those seeking a platform with straightforward course management and student engagement tools.

Try Teachable

Podia

Podia stands out as a dynamic online course platform that not only allows creators to develop and sell courses but also to build a community around their content. It’s designed for ease of access and follow-through, making it a great choice for both course creators and students.

Special Features:

  • Community Building: Podia emphasizes creating a community, providing tools to engage and interact with students.

  • Integrated Marketing: Tools for email marketing and other promotional activities are included.

  • Membership and Digital Downloads: Offers the ability to sell memberships and digital products alongside courses.

Pros:

  1. User-Friendly Interface: Simplifies course creation and management.

  2. All-in-One Platform: Combines courses, memberships, and marketing tools in one place.

  3. No Transaction Fees: Creators keep more of their earnings.

  4. Effective Community Tools: Facilitates interaction and engagement with students.

Cons:

  1. Limited Customization: While user-friendly, it may offer less customization than some competitors.

  2. Basic Analytics: The analytics tools may not be as advanced as those on other platforms.

  3. Email Marketing Limitations: While it includes marketing tools, they might not be as comprehensive as dedicated email marketing services.

In essence, Podia is an excellent choice for creators seeking a straightforward, all-in-one solution for online course creation, with a strong emphasis on community building.

Try Podia

Skool

Skool is designed to streamline the online learning experience. It integrates all the essentials needed to teach online into a user-friendly platform. With Skool, instructors can create and sell courses on a variety of topics, build a community, and track student progress. It’s particularly noted for its community-building features, including dedicated forums for each course where students and instructors can interact.

Special Features:

  • Community feature for team building and interaction.

  • Easy tracking of student progress.

  • Calendar feature for managing events.

  • Email broadcast for quick updates to students.

  • Chat, notifications, and profiles for managing different users.

Pros:

  • Extremely user-friendly and easy to learn.

  • Supports building a strong community, enhancing student engagement.

  • Effective administrative tools for posts, polls, and sharing resources.

  • Straightforward navigation with intuitive layout and design.

  • Offers a 14-day free trial to get started.

Cons:

  • Lacks its own video hosting capabilities, requiring external solutions for video content.

  • The simple payment plan might not suit every budget.

Skool’s focus on a simplified and integrated learning experience makes it a compelling choice for those looking for an accessible yet robust online course platform.

Try Skool

Mighty Networks

Mighty Networks is a dynamic online platform designed to build and grow thriving online communities around educational content. It stands out as a comprehensive tool for course creators, enabling them to integrate courses, membership sites, professional networks, podcasts, blogs, and even physical event spaces in one centralized location. This platform is especially adept at fostering engaging, interactive learning environments, leveraging the power of community and connectivity to enhance the educational experience.

Special Features

  • Live streaming and video uploads.

  • Mighty Effect™ personalization technology for content tailoring.

  • Welcome and Discovery sections for easy content navigation.

  • Unlimited membership capabilities.

  • Customizable landing pages and domains.

Pros

  • Accessible via mobile app for convenient learning.

  • Easy to create systems for remote organizations.

  • Comprehensive features for building and managing online communities.

Cons

  • Some challenges in integrating with external payment systems.

  • Requires a few extra steps to fully set up and integrate with other tools.

Mighty Networks provides an all-in-one solution for creators looking to build more than just courses, but whole communities and networks around their content.

Try Mighty Networks

LearnDash

LearnDash stands out as a highly versatile and feature-rich online course platform, particularly for those using WordPress. It’s a powerful plugin that seamlessly integrates with your WordPress site, enabling you to create, manage, and sell engaging online courses. LearnDash is celebrated for its user-friendly course builder, advanced quizzing capabilities, dynamic content delivery, and a range of monetization options. It’s especially popular among educators and businesses looking for a comprehensive Learning Management System (LMS) that offers deep customization and control over their online education offerings.

Special Features:

  • Focus Mode: A distraction-free learning environment for students.

  • Advanced Quizzing: Offers a variety of question types and quiz settings.

  • Drip-Feed Content: Allows for scheduled release of course content.

  • Dynamic Forums: Enables engaging community interactions.

  • Gradebook Technology: Mimics traditional school grading systems.

  • Course Points, Badges, and Certificates: Motivational tools for learners.

Pros:

  1. Integration with WordPress: Seamless integration makes it ideal for WordPress users.

  2. Customization: Highly customizable to fit various teaching styles and course requirements.

  3. User Engagement: Offers interactive tools like forums, badges, and certificates to enhance user engagement.

  4. Drip Content Feature: Allows for controlled release of content, enhancing the learning process.

  5. Strong Community and Support: Backed by a robust community and excellent customer support.

Cons:

  1. Learning Curve: Might be overwhelming for beginners due to its extensive features.

  2. Dependent on WordPress: Limits use to WordPress sites only, not suitable for those who use other platforms.

  3. Cost: While offering comprehensive features, it might be a bit pricey for individual educators or small businesses.

Try LearnDash

Pros:

Cons:

Try Ruzuku

This platform is particularly versatile, serving industries such as healthcare and insurance for ongoing education and training needs.

Special Features:

Why you should create an online course now!

1. Flexibility and Accessibility: One huge reason online courses are a hit is the flexibility they offer. As a creator, you can teach from anywhere, anytime. And for students? They get to learn at their own pace, in their pajamas if they want!

2. Wide Reach: The internet connects us globally, meaning your course can reach people from all corners of the world. It’s not just about teaching a few folks in a classroom; it’s about sharing your knowledge with thousands, maybe even millions!

3. Cost-Effective Learning and Teaching: Online courses often cost less to create and to enroll in compared to traditional education. This makes them super attractive to both teachers and learners.

4. The Pandemic Effect: Let’s face it, the COVID-19 pandemic pushed online learning from a nice-to-have to a must-have. It’s not just a trend; it’s the new normal in education.

5. Endless Topics: Whether you’re into baking, coding, yoga, or anything in between, there’s an audience waiting to learn from you. The variety of topics you can teach online is just endless.

6. Passive Income Potential: Once your course is up and running, it can earn you money while you sleep – talk about a sweet deal!
