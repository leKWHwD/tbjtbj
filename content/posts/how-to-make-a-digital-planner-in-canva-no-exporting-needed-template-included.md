### **1\. The Basics of making a digital planner with [Canva](https://thebeigejournal.com/Canva)**

Canva has made it incredibly easy to design digital planners. With its new feature that allows you to link pages, you no longer need to juggle multiple software. Everything can be done within Canva itself. Here's a basic structure to start with:

- **Cover Page**: The first impression of your planner.

- **Index or Home Page**: A quick navigation guide.

- **Yearly Calendar**: An overview of the year.

- **Monthly & Weekly Pages**: Detailed planning sections. Consider laying out five weeks for each month for flexibility.

### **2\. Hyperlinking: The Heart of Digital Planners**

The magic of digital planners lies in their interactivity. With Canva, you can:

- Link the yearly calendar to each month.

- Link each month to its corresponding weekly pages.

- Create tabs for quick navigation. For instance, tabs for each month, a home page icon, and a calendar page icon.

### **3\. Design Tips & Tricks**

- **Templates**: Canva offers a plethora of templates. While some are exclusive to Pro users, there are plenty available for free users as well. Use these as a starting point and customize to your liking.

- **Tables**: Canva's table feature is a game-changer. It functions like an Excel sheet, allowing you to create grids effortlessly.

- **Weekly Layouts**: Keep it simple. Use boxes and lines to structure your weeks. Remember, this is just the skeleton; you can always jazz it up later!

### **4\. Testing Your Digital Planner**

Once you've designed and linked your planner, it's crucial to test it. Download it as a PDF and check if all the links work correctly using any PDF reader. Ensure that each link takes you to the correct page.
