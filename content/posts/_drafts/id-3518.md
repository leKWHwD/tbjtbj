Gardening is a great way to spend time outside and enjoy the fresh air, but it can be a lot of work if you don’t have the right tools and knowledge. In this article, we’ll talk about when to add fertilizer to your garden, so you can keep your plants healthy and happy.

## What are fertilizers?

Fertilizers are chemicals or minerals that help plants grow. They are used to supplement the natural resources that a plant gets from the soil. There are three main types of fertilizers: organic, inorganic, and mineral. Organic fertilizers come from plants or animals, while inorganic fertilizers come from rocks, water, or air. Mineral fertilizers are made of micronutrients (such as nitrogen, phosphorus, potassium) and trace minerals (such as iron).

There are many different reasons to add fertilizer to your garden. One reason is to help the plant get enough nutrients to grow healthy and strong. Fertilizers also help the plant fight off diseases and pests. Fertilizers can be used before, during, or after transplanting a plant into the garden. It is important to test the soil before adding a fertilizer so you know what kind is best for your garden and soil type.

There are many different types of fertilizers available on the market today. It is important to research which one is best for your garden and situation. Some things to consider when choosing a fertilizer include: what kind of plant it is intended for (vegetables vs. flowers), how often it should be applied, and the cost.

## What types of fertilizers are there?

There are many types of fertilizers available to gardeners, each with its own advantages and disadvantages. Here is a brief overview of the most common types:

Organic fertilizers: These are made from natural sources, such as animal manure, compost, or kitchen scraps. They are considered to be more environmentally friendly than chemical fertilizers, but they may not be as effective at boosting plant growth.

Chemical fertilizers: These come in two main types: synthetic and organic. Synthetic fertilizers are made from chemicals such as nitrogen, phosphorus, and potassium. They are more effective than organic fertilizers at boosting plant growth, but they can also be harmful if used incorrectly.

Both organic and synthetic fertilizers can be applied in liquid or granular form. Liquid fertilizers can be poured directly on the ground or into irrigation systems, while granular fertilizers can be sprinkled on the ground or used in fertilizer bags.

## When should you add fertilizer to your garden?

When to add fertilizer to your garden depends on the type of plant you are growing and the time of year. Different plants need different levels of nutrients, so it is important to check with your local garden center or botanist for guidance on what type and amount of fertilizer to use.

In general, most fertilizers contain nitrogen, phosphorus, potassium, and magnesium. Nitrogen is important for plants that grow flowers and fruits, phosphorus helps with root development, potassium helps with flowering and fruit production, while magnesium is essential for healthy leaf and stem growth.

Some fertilizers can be applied in direct doses at specific times of the year; others can be incorporated into the soil over time as the seasons change. Many gardeners prefer to apply a balanced fertilizer in early summer as new growth begins to emerge; later in the season when leaves start to fall off and fruit starts to form; and finally in late winter or early spring before new growth resumes.

Remember to read the instructions that come with your fertilizer before using it; always consult with a professional if you have any questions about how to best care for your plants!

## How do you calculate how much fertilizer to add to your garden?

When to add fertilizer to your garden can be tricky. Calculating how much fertilizer to add to your garden every month is important, especially if you're trying to achieve specific results.

There are a few factors to consider when calculating how much fertilizer to add to your garden: the type of plant you're growing, the fertility of the soil, and the weather conditions.

Some plants require more nitrogen than others. A nitrogen-rich fertilizer will help increase the growth of leafy plants like tomatoes and kale. If you have a vegetable garden that produces mostly root crops, a less nitrogen-rich fertilizer may be more appropriate.

The weather also plays a role in when to fertilize your garden. In general, fertilizing your garden in the early parts of thegrowing season will help stimulate growth while fertilizing in the late part of the season will help preserve yields. However, there are exceptions to this rule so it's important to consult with an expert if you have any questions about when is the best time to fertilize your garden.

## What are the benefits of fertilizing your garden?

There are numerous benefits to fertilizing your garden, including increased yields of crops, improved soil health and a decrease in pests. Here are three key reasons to add fertilizer to your garden:

First and foremost, fertilizing your garden will help increase the output of crops. Over time, adding fertilizer will help your plants grow larger and more robust, yielding bigger harvests. Fertilizers containing NPK (nitrogen, phosphorus and potassium) help plants take up these nutrients in the soil, making them more available for the plant to use.

Second, fertilizing your garden can improve soil health. When you add compost and other organic matter to your garden amendment mix, you’re helping to break down nutrients into smaller molecules that are more easily absorbed by the plants. This process makes the soil more fertile, which in turn helps promote healthy plant growth.

Lastly, fertilizing your garden can help decrease pests. By supplying your plants with essential nutrients they need to thrive, you’re less likely to see pest problems develop. Fertilizer also helps to stimulate the growth of beneficial insects that feed on pests or prey on their eggs.
