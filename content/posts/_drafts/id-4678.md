Hey there, students! With a new semester in full swing, you're probably juggling assignments, extracurriculars, and maybe even a part-time job. But don't worry, I've got your back. Today, let's talk about 10 must-have apps and extensions that will make your life as a student in 2022 a whole lot easier.

## 1\. Daily Note: Your Personal Journal 📝

First up is **Daily Note**, a daily journaling app that's minimalistic and easy to use. Journaling can be therapeutic and helps you reflect on your day. You can even add a photo to your journal entry. If you're someone who struggles with keeping a physical journal, this app is a game-changer.

## 2\. Prompted Journal: Guided Reflection 🤔

If you find it hard to journal on a blank page, **Prompted Journal** is for you. This app gives you different prompts to reflect on, making it easier to engage in meaningful journaling. It's a great way to slow down and evaluate where you are in life.

## 3\. Grammarly: Your Writing Assistant 🖋️

We've all been there—last-minute essay writing. **[Grammarly](https://fave.co/47LY1GR)** is a digital writing assistant that helps you improve your writing style, vocabulary, and overall quality. It's free to download and integrates seamlessly with Google Docs. Say goodbye to those pesky grammar mistakes!

## 4\. Notion: The Ultimate Organizer 🗂️

**[Notion](https://thebeigejournal.com/notion)** is an all-in-one organization system. You can track your course syllabus, create to-do lists, and even manage your finances. It's especially useful at the start of a new semester when deadlines are easy to miss.

## 5\. Smart Tasks: To-Do List Simplified 📋

If you're looking for a straightforward to-do list app, **Smart Tasks** is your go-to. You can categorize tasks, color-code them, and even view all your completed tasks for that sweet sense of accomplishment.

## 6\. Google Drive Suite: More Than Just Storage 📦

**Google Drive** and its suite of apps like Google Docs and Google Sheets are not just for storage. You can use them for note-taking, tracking deadlines, and even creating study plans for exams.

## 7\. Video Speed Adjuster: Speed Up Your Learning 🎥

This extension is a lifesaver for those who want to watch lectures at speeds faster than the usual 2x. It's also useful for skimming through YouTube videos.

## 8\. Read Aloud: Text-to-Speech 🗨️

**Read Aloud** is a text-to-speech extension that can read any highlighted text on a webpage. It's particularly useful for reading textbooks more efficiently.

## 9\. Web Highlighter: Mark Important Info 🖍️

If you're doing complicated readings online, a **Web Highlighter** extension can help you mark important information, making it easier to review later.

## 10\. Idenati: Organize Your Web Life 🌐

Last but not least, **3** is an extension that helps you organize and categorize your frequently visited pages. It's also great for jotting down brief notes and to-do lists.

## Wrapping Up 🎁

So there you have it, folks! These apps and extensions are designed to make your life as a student more organized, productive, and stress-free. If you found this helpful, don't forget to like, subscribe, and check out more content from revisign on YouTube.
