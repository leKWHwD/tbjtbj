If you’re one of the many people who have resolved to start journaling in 2020, congratulations! Journaling is a great way to boost your mental health and well-being. But if you’ve never done it before, it can be hard to know where to start. Here are some journaling tips for beginners to help you get started on the right foot!

## Why journaling is beneficial

Journaling is a great way to get in touch with your emotions and thoughts. It can help you process what is going on in your life and can be therapeutic. Journaling can also help you set goals and track your progress. If you are new to journaling, here are some tips to get started.

Pick a journal that you like. There are many different types of journals available, so choose one that appeals to you. Consider factors such as the material the journal is made from, the size, and the style of binding.

Decide how often you want to journal. Some people journal every day, while others do it weekly or monthly. Find a frequency that works for you.

Set aside some time each day or week to write in your journal. Make sure this is a time when you will not be interrupted so that you can focus on your writing.

Start by writing about whatever is on your mind. You don't have to start with deep emotions or profound thoughts. Just write whatever comes to mind. Over time, you will likely find yourself venting less and exploring your feelings more.

Be honest with yourself. Journaling is a safe space for you to express your true thoughts

## How to get started with journaling

If you're new to journaling, it can be difficult to know where to start. Here are a few tips to help you get started:

1\. Find a comfortable place to write. It can be anywhere that makes you feel relaxed and safe.

2\. Set some time aside each day, or at least each week, to write in your journal. Dedicating even just 15 minutes to journaling can make a big difference.

3\. Don't worry about what you should write about. Just start writing whatever comes to mind. You can always go back and edit later if you want.

4\. Don't force yourself to write if you don't feel like it. Sometimes it's more important to just take a break and relax.

5\. If you get stuck, try prompts or prompts. Writing prompts can help you get started when you don't know what to write about.

Hopefully these tips will help you get started with journaling!

## Tips for making journaling a habit

Journaling can be a great way to process your thoughts and feelings, but it can be hard to make it a habit. Here are some tips to help you get started:

1\. Pick a time of day that you can stick to. Whether it’s first thing in the morning or right before bed, find a time that works for you and try to stick to it.

2\. Keep it simple. Don’t worry about writing long, eloquent entries – just write whatever comes to mind. The goal is to get your thoughts down on paper, not to produce Pulitzer-worthy prose.

3\. Make it a ritual. Create a simple ceremony around your journaling practice, such as lighting a candle or incense, saying a short prayer or affirmation, or taking a few deep breaths before you begin writing. This will help signal to your mind that it’s time to start journaling.

4\. Be patient with yourself. If you miss a day (or two, or three), don’t beat yourself up about it. Just pick up where you left off and keep going.

## What to write about in your journal

There are different ways to approach journaling, but here are some tips for beginners that can help you get started:

1\. Write about your day: What did you do today? How did you feel? What were your thoughts and emotions?

2\. Write about your goals: What do you want to achieve? Why do you want to achieve it? What steps will you take to get there?

3\. Write about your fears: What are you afraid of? Why are you afraid of it? What can you do to overcome your fear?

4\. Write about your happiest moments: What made you happy? Why were you happy in that moment? What can you do to replicate that feeling?

5\. Write about anything and everything that is on your mind: This can be a great way to just get everything out, and can help clear your head for the day or week ahead.

## How to find the time to journal

One of the biggest roadblocks to journaling is finding the time to do it. We lead busy lives and often feel like we don't have a minute to spare. However, journaling is a worthwhile activity that can offer many benefits. Here are some tips for finding the time to journal:

1\. Get up earlier: If you can find even 15-20 minutes in the morning to write in your journal, it can set the tone for the rest of the day. You may be surprised at how much easier it is to find time for other things when you get your journaling session in first thing.

2\. Go to bed later: If you have trouble falling asleep, spending some time journaling before bed can help you wind down and relax. It can also be a great way to reflect on your day and process any emotions that may be keeping you up at night.

3\. Make use of waiting time: We often have pockets of time throughout the day where we're waiting for something or someone. Instead of scrolling through social media or playing games on your phone, use this time to pull out your journal and write a few lines.

4\. Set aside specific journaling time: Just likeyou would schedule any other important activity, put journaling time in your calendar. This will help you make it a priority and ensure that you actually do it.

5\. Keep it simple: You don't need to write War and Peace every time you sit down to journal. Just a few sentences about what's on your mind can be beneficial. The important thing is to get into the habit of writing regularly.

## Conclusion

There are endless benefits to journaling, and it's a great activity for anyone who wants to explore their thoughts and feelings in a safe, private space. If you're new to journaling, these tips will help you get started. Choose a journal that appeals to you, set aside some time each day (or week) to write, and let your thoughts flow onto the page. Don't worry about perfection – just let yourself go and see what comes out. You may be surprised at how therapeutic and insightful journaling can be.
