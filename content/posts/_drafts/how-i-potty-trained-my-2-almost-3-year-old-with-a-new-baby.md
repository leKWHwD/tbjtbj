Like every parent's journey, ours was different. We have been pretty relaxed with potty training because my Daughter was looked after by family. We didn’t need to go to daycare or prepare for preschool, but then when she was around 2 1/2 years old, I wanted to start signing her up for classes, and a lot of them required her to be potty trained.

> And that’s when I had a little panic attack. She was already at the stage that she knew what she wanted and very well into her terrible twos. She, like most toddlers was stubborn, so getting her to do some thing that she didn’t want to was hard.

So I started doing my research on what party train and followed any Instagram account that was potty training related just to get myself prepared. At this point I was already feeling like we were very behind, and most kids her age were already trained or was on their way. I was definitely feeling the mom guilt for not starting her earlier.

> Side note
> 
> At around two we started her with pull-ups because who wants to wrestle a toddler to change a diaper. this is definitely not necessary to start potty training but I think it helps start little transitions.
> 
> We were going to have another baby, so we need you to hold off training While she was getting used to a new sibling. But mostly I secretively liked her in diapers because I didn’t want to look for washrooms while we are out.

## The introduction

You’ve actually bought parties for her a while back but I’ve never forced her to use it. They were generally around the house open for her to explore them and once in a while she would get interested and put her toys on there or she would try to sit on there which was actually really nice to see because she wasn’t afraid of it. And then around 2 1/2 years old we started talking to her that she should try going to the potty and one of the potty‘s.

## How we started

This was definitely not a linear journey. Like most things with toddlers, things progress and then regress.

At this point she has seen a lot of Cocomelon so she knows of the potty song and the phrases that they use to describe going to the washroom. So we start first started off asking her if she had a _funny feeling_ and if she needed to go to the washroom. Most of the time her answer would be no but once in a while she would say yes and then we would quickly run and get the potty so she can sit down and have her pee.

Fast forward a few weeks of this and suddenly she decided she had no interest in the potty and developed an aversion to it. So after this we took a few weeks off to ease the pressure.

## The research (rabbit hole) starts

During this time the panic started and I thought that we’re in trouble.

My daughter is almost 3 and still in diapers.

This is also when my Research and buying spree started. I bought everything from training pants, sticker charts, potty training courses… you name it. I felt like if I had everything then she would magically be able to pee in a potty right?

Especially during my research in mommy groups (haha) A lot of people recommended this potty training book, called **[Oh crap potty training](https://amzn.to/3wtyQYs)**. I’m sure you’ve heard of it it’s pretty famous.

A lot of people claimed that this is the book to use, but for me it was just too much dedication to have my eyes on my child the whole day watching for signs for her to pee and rushing her to the potty.

Which means staying at home for three days straight without leaving the house, on top of attending to a new baby.

This is just not the stress i needed. So I decided against the advice from the mom groups.

And just to note, what they say about getting new underwear, the sticker charts, the rewards, it did not work. She did not care about the sticker, she did not care about getting new undies, she only went to the washroom because she could have a piece of candy or else there was no deal.

I didn’t think the candy bribery was sustainable, So we didn’t go that route. We also tried screen time during party, but that was just too distracting for her to concentrate on the task at hand. She would just sit on the toilet and watch her shows without going.

## Light at the end of the tunnel

So after a few months of this wishy washy training, and following her lead, there was light at the end of the tunnel.

She was progressively more aware of her body sensations. She would tell you that she needed to go or had a funny feeling, and then subsequently just go in her diapers. Which was a step up from not announcing it before. At times like that, we would try to rush her to the potty as quick as we can before she went in her diapers, or if she announced it after she went in her diapers we would acknowledge her good work for identifying her feelings.

And then a few more months of this went on where she would announce her sensations and either we make a mad dash to the potty, or have her go in the diaper.

So we decided to peel off the rest of the Band-Aid by having her go commando most of the time at home. We felt like she was using the diaper as a safety blanket. It’s sometime felt like she was going in the diaper and not letting us change the diaper because it was comforting and secure for her. And she would not go in the potty because she didn’t really have to.

At first we removed her diaper and put on training pants but because the training pants Were thick, and kind of felt like diapers she would have a lot of accidents in them. So that is why we went with nothing instead of training pants.

And once that started she was more communicative about her needs BEFORE relieving herself. In the beginning there were a few accidents but after that she learned that wet pants and pee on her legs did not feel good so she knew to hold her pee. And after a few days there were close to no accidents.

## Potty trained-ish

Our potty training journey had a lot of ups and downs and progression and regression. But in the end we made it - kind of.

I said kind of because we have only tackle the P portion and not the poop proportion.

But I will take this victory any day because now she knows who communicate her sensations. I have read that going number two on the toilet takes a longer time to tackle. So for now we will stick with diapers still for her poop. We are now at the stage where when she needs to go number two she will ask for a diaper. And we’re totally fine with that.

As for naps and night sleep, we still put a diaper on her just in case of accidents. If we know that she will nap in a car we will put a pull-up on her before heading out.

When we go out we now tell her to go before you go which is a phrase from Cocomelon. And she understands that she needs to go potty before heading out so she doesn’t need to wear a diaper. She now wears undies and dislike the feeling of diapers because it’s so dry and uncomfortable compared to cotton undies.

I have bought her a **[portable potty](https://amzn.to/3PPlSLx)** to go when we are out but we have yet to use it outside because she generally can hold her pee the whole time, which is not always good.

So if she wants to go when we are out we are still using pull-ups to catch her during her little washroom break. And she’s completely fine with that and works with us so I’m not complaining!

## Takeaway

Everybody’s potty training journey looks a little bit different, And that’s completely OK. I hope my story helps with your potty training efforts.

> What we learned from this is that they are always ready at their own pace. I think by naturally guiding her and following her lead, she was able to catch up to her abilities in expressing herself, and her body was able to respond to her needs.

If you ever felt like there’s no hope in your potty train journey, just know that it will happen, just as a different pace than others.
