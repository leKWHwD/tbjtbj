So you’ve taken the leap to be a creativepreneur and finally set up shop on Etsy — Congrats!That was all easy, but now your question is, “Why aren’t I making any sales? I haven’t had anybody visit my page for the last week let alone make a sale!”If you want to be successful on Etsy, there are a few more things you need to do. And the first thing is — you can’t just set it and forget it!Etsy has massive traffic ([170M visits in the last 6 months](https://www.blogger.com/blog/post/edit/8025534002846347833/7576827523899440146#)) but here are a few things you can do to drive some of that traffic to you shop.

## **Learn about Etsy’s SEO**

In case you don’t know what that means, it means Search Engine Optimization. That’s right, you need to name your products in an optimal way so that it’s relevant to what your buyers are searching for on Etsy! That means don’t want to name your product “greeting cards” when your buyers are more likely to search for “unique birthday cards”.Read more on [4 Ways to Improve Your SEO and Increase Your Traffic](https://www.blogger.com/blog/post/edit/8025534002846347833/7576827523899440146#)

## **2\. Be proactive and connect with your Buyers**

Show your appreciation for your buyers with a “thank you” coupon code in your automated messages after a purchase under “Message to Buyers”. It will allow your buyers to feel appreciated and your fans to make repeat purchases!Read more about [Etsy’s Seller Policy here about Communications](https://www.blogger.com/blog/post/edit/8025534002846347833/7576827523899440146#)

## **3\. Get social on Etsy’s Community**

Etsy has it’s own internal community made up of “[Teams](https://www.blogger.com/blog/post/edit/8025534002846347833/7576827523899440146#)” and “[Forums](https://www.blogger.com/blog/post/edit/8025534002846347833/7576827523899440146#)”. You can make or join your own Teams to connect with fellow Etsy Sellers or you can contribute to the Forum. There are often Forum posts that will allow you to promote your shop!

## **4\. Spend a little money**

Spending money before making money doesn’t sound like something you want to do when you’re just starting your shop. But a little spending/ investment on the right things, can go a long way. You can [promote your Etsy listing](https://www.blogger.com/blog/post/edit/8025534002846347833/7576827523899440146#) with a maximum daily so you can have control of how much you spend (from $1 to $10). You can also control your Maximum Cost Per Click so you don’t blow your budget on just one click. Promoting my Etsy items was what changed my shop from being another dud to becoming a thriving shop. It also gives great analytics and valuable insights for your shop. [Learn more about managing your Etsy promoted listings here](https://www.blogger.com/blog/post/edit/8025534002846347833/7576827523899440146#).
