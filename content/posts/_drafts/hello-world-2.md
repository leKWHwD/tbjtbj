## Tips for sticking to the schedule

It can be difficult to stick to a schedule with a toddler, but there are some tips that can help. First, it's important to be consistent with the schedule. If there are days when the schedule has to be different, try to explain this to your toddler in advance.

Second, make sure the activities on the schedule are things that your toddler enjoys. If they're not, they're likely to resist and make it harder to stick to the schedule.

Finally, be flexible and willing to adjust the schedule as needed. If your toddler is having a hard time with a particular activity, try something else or take a break.

## Toddler-friendly activities to include in the daily routine

During the day, include some active playtime to help them release energy and promote a healthy lifestyle.

Incorporating healthy snacks and meals into the day will also help keep them on track.

And finally, allow some time for quiet activities such as reading or puzzles before bedtime to help them wind down. By following these tips, you can create a daily routine that works for both you and your toddler.

## When to start implementing a daily routine

There is no one-size-fits-all answer to this question, as the best time to start implementing a daily routine with your toddler will vary depending on your child's age and temperament.

However, in general, it is usually best to start gradually introducing some structure into your child's day around the age of 18 months. This can be as simple as having regular mealtimes and nap times, and gradually adding in additional activities such as outdoor play or story time as your child gets older.

If you are unsure about when to start implementing a daily routine with your toddler, talk to your child's pediatrician for guidance.

## Conclusion

It’s important to remember that each child is different and therefore, schedules will look different from family to family. What works for one toddler, might not work for another. The most important thing is to be flexible and go with what works best for your toddler and your family. Also, don’t forget to have some fun along the way!
