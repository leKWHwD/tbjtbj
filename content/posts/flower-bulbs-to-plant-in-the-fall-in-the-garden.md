## How to plant and care for your flower bulbs

Fall is the perfect time to plant flower bulbs in your garden. Here are a few tips to help you get started:

1. Choose the right location. Flower bulbs need well-drained soil and full sun. If you have heavy clay soil, consider adding sand to improve drainage.
2. Prepare the soil. Loosen the soil to a depth of about 8 inches and mix in some compost or other organic matter.
3. Plant the bulbs. Plant the bulbs pointy side up at a depth of about three times the height of the bulb. Water well after planting and mulch with a few inches of straw or leaves.
4. Fertilize and water regularly. Once the bulbs start to grow, fertilize them with a balanced fertilizer and water regularly. Be sure to keep the soil moist but not soggy.
5. deadhead flowers as they fade. Deadheading (removing spent flowers) will encourage your bulbs to produce more blooms. Simply snip off the faded flowers at the base of the stem.

With a little care, your flower bulbs will provide beautiful blooms all spring long!
